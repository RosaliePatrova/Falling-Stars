Falling Stars

Once upon a time, in the distant corners of the universe, two stars were born. One was called Lyra, a shimmering, radiant star that blazed with
the intensity of a thousand suns. The other was Orion, a soft yet steady glow in the sky, whose light carried a calm and eternal warmth. From the very moment they were born,
they were destined to shine in the same universe, though they would never truly touch. Lyra and Orion spent their days and nights tracing their own paths in the cosmos, unaware of one another's existence. They burned brightly in the same sky, separated by the vast emptiness of space. Yet, when the wind of the stars carried whispers through the galaxies, they began to hear of one another. Lyra's light danced with the cosmic winds, and she looked up at the stars around her. But there was something different about the way Orion's light flickered in her mind. It was gentle, constant, and familiar, like the soft lullaby sung to a child in the quiet of the night. She began to wonder, who was this star that filled her thoughts with such a quiet joy? Orion, too, felt a tug in his heart as his light shimmered through the endless stretches of space. He had always been alone, glowing steadily and never wavering, yet something about Lyra's brilliance stirred him. It was as though her light was a distant echo of his own, and in that distant echo, he found a feeling he had never known—longing. They could not touch, could not even speak, for the distance between them was unmeasurable, and the silence of space was a vast ocean. Yet, every time their rays crossed paths in the night sky, they felt a deep connection, as though their very essence danced in harmony. One evening, as the stars began to fade and the universe slowly spun into a new era, a strange thing occurred. The fabric of the universe began to shift, and the forces of time and space pulled them in opposite directions. It was as if the universe, in its infinite wisdom, decided that they could not remain close. The very distance that had once kept them apart was now becoming an insurmountable wall. Lyra’s light dimmed, and Orion’s glow flickered as they felt the pull of the cosmic tides. They could feel each other’s warmth, but the distance grew, ever widening, until one fateful moment when they were no longer able to see each other’s light. Their hearts, bound by the vastness of space, ached in the silence. But despite the separation, they never forgot each other. The stars are immortal, and their love, like the universe itself, is infinite. Lyra and Orion continued to burn brightly in the night sky, their lights now far apart but forever intertwined in the celestial dance of the heavens. The universe had pulled them apart, but in the deep silence of space, they remained connected by something greater—a love that could never be extinguished.
